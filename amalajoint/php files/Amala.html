Amala

Amala is a traditional staple food for the part of Yoruba tribe in southwestern part of Nigeria. It's a type of swallow, which is a staple food in southwestern part of Nigeria. Amala is has a dark brownish colour. Texture-wise, it is firmer than mashed potatoes because of its low moisture and high starch content. The ingredients for making elubo( the flour), for amala are sundried, so, they lose most of their moisture. They, however, retain their nutrients. Most elubo flours are rich in fibre and protein. Swallows are typically served as accompaniments with favorite soups. Ingredient.


The major ingredients are

1. 2 (two) cups of boiling water
2. 3 cups of flour (yam flour, cassava flour or plantain flour).


Ingredient Source

1. Online (e.g Amazon)
2. Any localized African shop


Method of preparation

Step 1: Sieve the flour
Step 2: Bring water to a boil on stove
Step 3: Take out one cup of the boiled water for use later
Step 4: Add the flour to remaining water in pot and stir to combine fully
Step 5: Make a hole in center of the dough formed and pour in half a cup of the water you took out earlier on and cover the pot for about 2-3 minutes then, stir again for about a minute or two.
Step 6: Feel the texture if it is right for you, you are done, if not add a little more water if you want it softer.
Step 7: Serve: ready to eat Shape the dough into balls or portions and serve with your favorite Nigerian soup or stew. Common accompaniments include Egusi soup, Ewedu soup, Gbegiri soup, or Okra soup.
Step 8: Enjoy: Amala is typically eaten by tearing off small portions with your fingers and using them to scoop up the soup or stew.
