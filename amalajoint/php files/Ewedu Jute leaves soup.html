Jute Leaves (Ewedu) Soup

Ewedu soup, a traditional Nigerian dish prevalent among the Yoruba ethnic group, is made from jute leaves and pairs well with starchy sides like amala or pounded yam. The soup is prepared by boiling and blending the leaves, then seasoning with crayfish, locust beans, and spices before simmering. Its distinct earthy and slightly bitter taste contributes to the rich flavors of Nigerian cuisine, making it a flavorful and authentic dish to savor alongside other traditional Nigerian foods.


The major ingredients are

1. 1 (two) cups of boiling water
2. Jute leaves (Ewedu)
3. Locust beans
4. Salt


Ingredient Source

1. Online (e.g Amazon)
2. Any localized African shop


Method of preparation

Step 1: Separate/pluck the leaves from the jute tree one at a time
Step 2: Rinse the leaves thouroughly to remove the sand and dirt from leaves step 3: Bring water to a boil in a port on stove
Step 4: Add the leaves to the water and cook for about 8 - 10 minutses or till the jute leaves become tender
step 5: At the tender stage add crayfish, locust beans and salt. Step 6: Blend the ewedu using blender or traditional small broom called Ijabe in Nigerian language
step 7: Return the blended ewedu leaves to the pot and place it back on the stove for about 2 mins depending on quantity. Add ground crayfish, locust beans (iru), salt, pepper, and any other seasoning of your choice. Stir well to combine the ingredients.
